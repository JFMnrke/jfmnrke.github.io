# Case Study 1

# Standardizing Campaign Activation Across Multiple Regions

## Context

Amazon Prime Video campaigns were being activated using information distributed across multiple sources:

* Media plans
* Copy documents
* Creative asset folders
* Email threads
* Audience descriptions
* Regional workflows

Each region had developed its own way of working, creating inconsistencies, misunderstandings, and operational inefficiencies.

---

## The Problem

Campaign teams spent significant time gathering and validating information before implementation.

Key information such as:

* Audiences
* URLs
* Creative assets
* Copy
* Budgets

could arrive through different channels depending on the market.

As a result:

* Implementation times varied dramatically between regions.
* Communication overhead increased.
* Activation errors became more likely.
* Campaign launches were slower than necessary.

---

## My Role

I created and led a documentation initiative involving representatives from multiple regions.

To understand the problem thoroughly, I:

* Interviewed team members.
* Mapped regional workflows.
* Documented pain points.
* Compared implementation processes across markets.

I also partnered with the Employee Wellbeing initiative because I believed operational improvements would directly improve team experience and reduce unnecessary stress.

---

## The Solution

After researching existing workflows, I proposed consolidating campaign information into a unified activation framework.

The goal was simple:

### Create a Single Source of Truth

Containing:

* Audience definitions
* Copy
* URLs
* Creative assets
* Campaign requirements
* Implementation instructions

This eliminated the need to search through multiple documents and email threads before activation.

---

## Challenges

Not all markets worked the same way.

Mexico already had a more advanced process called the **Activation Sheet** and initially resisted the proposed changes.

Finding a balance between existing regional processes and a scalable global framework required stakeholder alignment, training, and phased implementation.

---

## Implementation

The rollout was conducted in phases:

* Regional strategy teams received training.
* Support sessions were provided.
* Adoption deadlines were established.
* Teams were guided through the transition process.

---

## Impact

The initiative reduced campaign preparation time from several hours in some markets to a more standardized and efficient workflow.

Implementation times became more consistent across regions.

Some teams that previously required several hours to prepare and activate campaigns were able to complete the process much more efficiently.

### The project improved:

* Activation speed
* Information visibility
* Cross-team collaboration
* Process consistency
* Overall operational efficiency

---

## Key Learning

> The best operational improvements begin by listening.

Understanding how people actually work is often more valuable than designing processes in isolation.
