# Case Study 2
## Audience Discovery & Performance Optimization Through Data-Driven Budget Allocation

### Context

Across multiple Amazon Prime Video Canada campaigns, my role involved monitoring campaign performance, identifying optimization opportunities, and translating audience insights into strategic recommendations.

Rather than treating campaign structures as fixed, I continuously analyzed audience behavior, creative performance, platform efficiency, and engagement trends to identify opportunities for improvement.

### The Challenge

Many campaigns launched with assumptions about:

- Who the audience was
- Which formats would perform best
- Where budget should be allocated

However, audience behavior often revealed a different reality once campaigns were live.

The challenge was not simply optimizing delivery, but understanding where actual demand existed and reallocating investment accordingly.

---

## Betty La Fea: Identifying an Unexpected Core Audience

The campaign initially launched with a broad audience structure using a single ad set.

As performance data accumulated, I noticed engagement patterns suggesting stronger interest among older audiences than originally anticipated.

To validate this hypothesis, I recommended restructuring the campaign into three audience groups:

- Broad audience (control group)
- Older audience segment
- Younger audience segment

This allowed us to isolate performance and better understand audience behavior.

The results showed that older audiences were generating stronger engagement and viewership metrics.

Based on these findings, budget allocation was adjusted to prioritize the higher-performing segment, improving overall campaign efficiency while generating valuable audience insights for future campaigns.

---

## NHL: Expanding Beyond Traditional Market Assumptions

NHL campaigns were originally focused on regions directly associated with participating teams.

While analyzing performance across markets, I observed strong engagement signals from regions that were not part of the original targeting strategy.

One example was Manitoba, where audience interest remained high even when local teams were not directly involved in the promoted games.

Based on these findings, I proposed expanding campaign coverage to additional regions where audience demand existed.

The approach proved successful and eventually contributed to broader regional targeting strategies across future campaigns.

---

## Fallout Season 2 & Other Entertainment Campaigns: Format Reallocation Strategy

Several campaigns relied on formats that were generating weaker efficiency compared to emerging alternatives.

By monitoring performance trends, I identified opportunities to reduce investment in underperforming formats such as carousel placements and certain static executions.

At the same time, I observed stronger efficiency from newer and less competitive formats, including interactive experiences on Snapchat and high-impact placements across other platforms.

I recommended reallocating budget toward the higher-performing formats, allowing campaigns to generate stronger outcomes while maintaining spending efficiency.

The strategy was applied across multiple entertainment campaigns, including:

- Fallout Season 2
- Wrecking Crew
- Other entertainment launches

---

## Female-Led Content Initiative: Challenging Audience Assumptions

One campaign was designed around content traditionally targeted toward women.

Initial audience planning heavily prioritized female targeting.

However, platform reporting revealed meaningful engagement from male audiences in placements where gender restrictions were not available.

By investigating the data further, I identified an opportunity to broaden audience strategy while maintaining campaign relevance.

The campaign evolved from a predominantly female-focused approach toward a more balanced audience model, allowing us to capture previously overlooked demand and improve overall reach potential.

---

## Impact

These optimizations consistently followed the same principle:

> Do not optimize based on assumptions.  
> Optimize based on observed behavior.

By using campaign data to validate or challenge initial hypotheses, I was able to:

- Identify audience opportunities
- Improve budget allocation decisions
- Increase campaign efficiency
- Provide strategic recommendations that influenced future planning decisions

---

## Key Learning

Performance optimization is not only about improving metrics.

It is about understanding people.

The most valuable opportunities often appear when data challenges what we originally believed about an audience, a platform, or a campaign strategy.
